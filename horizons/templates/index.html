Welcome to Aakash's Astronomy Site

This is my Astronomy site where I publish pictures of planets that I have taken

This is a video I got of a near Earth asteroid that passed over Davis, California. I mainly use my Newtonian Reflector to take pictures at night. A Newtonian reflector uses a back mirror to reflect light into an eyepiece which futher magnifies the image. These asteroids can be seen when there are minimal light sources in the area surrounding you. You want to have a telescope that can actively track objects in the night sky. I would personally recommend using JPL horizons for any pathfinding that you may need to do.

A little bit about myself ...

I have worked in the past with vision tracking using limelights on my robotics team as well as Open CV. Last summer, I worked on studying asteroids at MIT by creating python programs that processed FITS files and other data from Palomar Observatory. I was also able to publish this research. For the last two years, I have programmed for my robotics team in c++ and some Python as well. I have experience with making my own star tracking programs which I created as a part of my project to make a computerized telescope.
